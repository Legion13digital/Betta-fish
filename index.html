About betta fish

Betta splendens, also known as Siamese fighting fish, are freshwater fish native to Southeast Asia. They are known for their bright colors and large, beautiful fins.

These fish have a labyrinthine organ that allows them to breathe atmospheric air. Males are especially bright and often exhibit aggressive behavior towards other males.

Features of care

For a comfortable life, Bettas requires an aquarium with a volume of at least 5 liters with a temperature of 24-28 Â°C. It is important to provide them with a varied diet and regular water changes.

The average life expectancy of Betta is 2-4 years. They are known for their territorial nature, especially males, who do not tolerate proximity to each other.

The natural environment

In nature, Betta live in the slow-flowing waters of Southeast Asia - in Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam and Malaysia.

Their natural environment is rice paddies, shallow streams, and swampy areas with abundant vegetation.

Aquarium conditions

In an aquarium, it is important to recreate conditions close to natural ones: a soft substrate, live plants and shelters.

It is necessary to maintain a stable temperature and avoid strong water flow.

Compatibility

Male Betta should be kept separately. Females can be kept in groups with a sufficient volume of the aquarium.

From the neighbors, quiet views are suitable: neons, corydoras, ampullaries.
